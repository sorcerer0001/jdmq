      Jin Ding Animation Studio LLC is incorporated in Beijing. The creative team is composed of talented young artists guided by experienced senior artists who have been working for several decades in Chinese fine arts, animation & film industry.

      Jin Ding Animation Studio LLC believes that originality is the highest form of creative capital, and has the insight & ability to develop high quality subject matter. We extend the animation industry chain and expand the market space by building our own brands.

      Jin Ding Animation Studio LLC is committed to portray unique and interesting characters within the vibrant & colorful scenarios of Chinese art. Our animated TV series, TALES FROM JOURNEY TO THE WEST, demonstrates our ability and has received popular attention & critical praise. This series is not only distinguished as a collaborative project with China’s CCTV, but has also received positive reception from overseas producers.

Xiangqian Ma（Producer）

      Mr. Ma was formerly the editor of children’s education & literature publications and the director of children’s programming at CCTV. In 1984, Mr. Ma was employed by China’s State Administration of Radio Film & Television. After years of involvement with children’s animation broadcasting & marketing, he published an article entitled “The Crisis and Countermeasure of Chinese Animation”, which won a first prize for excellent publication in China.
      In 1995, Mr. Ma became director of the president’s office of the Hong Kong Department of Light International, Inc., and also co-producer for a co-production between China’s National Film Channel and Paramount Studios.
      In 2001, Mr. Ma was appointed as president of the Beijing Shi Dai Dong Hua Media Company and vice-president of America United Media Management LLC. He also served as producer on various television programs, was director of the awards office secretary and master of ceremonies committee of the China Radio & Television Association, vice-chairman of the China Film & Audio Association, and vice-chairman of the China Youth Friendship Environment Association.
Min Wu（Director, Chief Art Director & Character Designer）

      Ms. Wu is a nationally ranked Chinese artist who was formerly employed at the Beijing Science & Educational Film Studio, and worked as an animation director at CCTV for more than 20 years. Ms. Wu is also a guest professor in the School of Design at the China Central Academy of Fine Arts, and the School of Art at the Beijing Electronics Science & Technology Institute.
      Ms. Wu has worked in children’s art education and publications for more than 10 years, producing several children’s cartoons and publishing various comic books such as “Qi Qi Cruiser Adventure”. The animated films and TV series that she has been involved with earned many governmental & industrial awards such as the Huabiao Award and the China Television Golden Eagle Award.
Yanan Chen（Associate Producer）

       Ms. Chen is a nationally-ranked Chinese artist with more than 25 years of professional experience as an artist, animator and producer. She has worked as an artist, designer, animator and production manager on more than one hundred animation episodes, which have aired on China’s CCTV over the past few years. Ms. Chen has also worked on Australian and Japanese animation projects. She is familiar with all processes of animation production, and excels at efficient budgeting, production management and cost control.
Kemei Cheng（Artist）

      Mr. Cheng holds a graduate degree from the China Central Academy of Fine Arts, where he is also is a vice-professor. Mr. Cheng was dean of studies at the China Central Academy of Fine Arts High School, and is now in charge of the Entertainment Study Office in the School of Design at the China Central Academy of Fine Arts and School of Art. He is also a contract artist with many galleries in Hong Kong and North America, and has a high reputation in the plastic arts.
Qiang Zhang（Production Control）

      Mr. Zhang holds a graduate degree from the China Central Academy of Fine Arts. He is now the Dean of Animation Department in Beijing Institute of Electronic Science and Technology.

Yuan Zong
      Written in the 16th century, it is a stunningly rich and innovative novel that deftly interweaves fantasy and realism, drama and comedy. The tale has always had an unparalleled hold on the imaginations and affections of generations of Chinese children, and serves as a guidebook for living to many. Journey to the West is one of the great classics of Eastern literature.
      However, despite the unique appeal of Journey to the West for young audiences, there has not yet been an animation made for young children that is designed to stimulate their mental and imaginative development.
      The unique value of our animated series, TALES FROM JOURNEY TO THE WEST, is to fill that gap in the market: to offer a charming, engaging version of Journey to the West for children aged 3-9, and their parents.
